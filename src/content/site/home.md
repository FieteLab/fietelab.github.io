Our group develops and adapts cutting-edge approaches from mathematics,
machine learning, and physics to analyze learning, dynamics,
computation, efficiency, and robustness of computation in the brain.

We wish to uncover how high-level cognitive function *emerges* from the
bottom up. What aspects of circuit architecture drive the emergence of
function from simple constituents? What are the properties of memory
systems constructed from noisy, leaky neurons, and how are limitations
of the building blocks overcome? What are the structural biases — such
as modularity — in brains that make them efficient and robust learners?

We find that the brain contains neural circuits with invariant
low-dimensional dynamics that underlie fundamental computations like
integration, surprising new analog error-correcting codes that enable
fault-tolerant computation, and ways in which the brain outperforms
modern AI in learning speed, data efficiency, and robustness — and we
seek to transfer these insights to build better machine intelligence.
