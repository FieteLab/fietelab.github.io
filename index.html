Fiete Lab

Our goal is to better understand the dynamics and coding principles that underlie computation in the brain.

MIT Department of Brain and Cognitive Sciences  ·  McGovern Institute for Brain Research

About the lab

We use mathematics, machine learning, and physics to study neural learning, circuit dynamics, and computational efficiency. Our central question is how high-level cognitive function emerges from the bottom up — and what structural properties enable robust, fault-tolerant brain function.

Research themes

A few of the directions we are pursuing.

  • Low-dimensional neural dynamics underlying fundamental computations
  • Memory systems built from imperfect neurons
  • Structural biases (e.g. modularity) that support efficient learning
  • Analog error-correcting codes enabling fault-tolerant computation
  • Comparative advantages of biological learning over modern AI
